# Intellectual Puns — for the "z" keyword

I would tell you a joke about an infinite series, but I don't know where to start.
I tried to write a joke about entropy, but it all fell apart.
Schrodinger's cat walks into a bar. And doesn't.
A photon checks into a hotel. The bellhop asks if he has luggage. The photon says: I'm traveling light.
I have a joke about construction, but I'm still working on it.
Why do programmers prefer dark mode? Because light attracts bugs.
I told a joke about noble gases once. No reaction.
The rotation group O(3) walks into a bar. The bartender says: we don't serve your type here. It says: that's not very symmetric of you.
Pavlov is sitting at a bar. The phone rings. He says: oh no, I forgot to feed the dogs.
Einstein, Newton, and Pascal are playing hide and seek. Einstein counts. Pascal hides in a bush. Newton draws a square on the ground and stands in it. Einstein opens his eyes and says: Newton, I found you. Newton says: actually you found one Newton per square meter — you found Pascal.
There are 10 types of people in the world: those who understand binary and those who don't.
Entropy isn't what it used to be.
A Roman walks into a bar, holds up two fingers and says: five beers please.
How do you comfort a JavaScript developer? You console them.
I asked the librarian if they had books about paranoia. She whispered: they're right behind you.
What do you call a fake noodle? An impasta.
Oxygen and magnesium went on a date. OMg.
Why can't you trust an atom? Because they make up everything.
I used to hate facial hair, but then it grew on me.
Did you hear about the mathematician who's afraid of negative numbers? He'll stop at nothing to avoid them.
The bartender says we don't serve time travelers here. A time traveler walks into a bar.
Light travels faster than sound. That's why some people appear bright until you hear them speak.
I'm reading a book about anti-gravity. It's impossible to put down.
A biologist, a chemist and a statistician go hunting. The biologist shoots at a deer and misses 5 feet to the left. The chemist shoots and misses 5 feet to the right. The statistician yells: we got him!
Why do scientists rarely tell jokes? Because their jokes always bomb.
